## Game Description

HOLE is a **low-resolution, single-player FPS extraction shooter** with a strong focus on gun jamming mechanics and raw, difficult gunfighting. The tagline is:

> "Casual & Hard FPS. Be careful with jam."

The core loop involves:
1. Accessing a **floating microwave oven** and jumping through the "hole" to enter a stage.
2. Surviving infinite enemy spawns in a dangerous environment.
3. Finding the microwave oven again to **extract** — i.e., jump back through the hole.

---

## Core Gameplay Features

### Extraction Mechanic
- Players enter and exit stages through a floating microwave oven (the "hole").
- There is **no time limit** — players decide when to extract.
- The goal is to survive as long as possible without losing equipment (unlike many extraction shooters, there is **no penalty for dying/losing gear** — explicitly noted as a design philosophy to allow casual play).

### Gun Jamming System
- The game features a realistic gun jamming mechanic.
- Players must manually **clear jams** mid-combat.
- The description humorously says: *"Let's clear jam with swearing."*
- This is a central tension element — the player must stay calm and fix jams under fire.

### Stage Structure
- **3 stages total** — only 1 unlocked at the start.
- Enemies spawn **infinitely** — there is no stage "clear."
- **Play time per stage:** 5–30 minutes.
- Estimated total unlock time: **2–3 hours** to unlock all stages.

### Weapons
- **4 guns total** — only 1 available at start.
- Additional guns must be unlocked through play.

### Difficulty Philosophy
- Described as both "casual" and "hard."
  - *Casual* — no story, no equipment loss on death, short sessions.
  - *Hard* — intense gunfighting, jam management, infinite enemies.
- Explicitly targeted at players who want "hard FPS without fear of losing equipment."

## Design Pillars (Inferred)

Based on the store page and community reception, HOLE appears to be built on:

1. **Minimalism** — strip away story, progression bloat, and complexity. Focus on the core loop.
2. **Tension through failure** — gun jams force the player to manage panic.
3. **Freedom without punishment** — no loot loss on death makes it approachable.
4. **Surreal horror aesthetics** — low-fi visuals amplify discomfort rather than reduce it.
5. **Short-burst replayability** — stages designed for 5–30 min sessions.

## Comparison to Extraction Shooter Genre

HOLE occupies a unique niche within extraction shooters:

| Feature | HOLE | Traditional Extraction Shooters (e.g., Tarkov) |
|---------|------|--------------------------------------------------|
| Multiplayer | No | Yes (PvPvE) |
| Loot loss on death | No | Yes |
| Session length | 5–30 min | 30–90+ min |
| Story | None | Often present |
| Price | ~$1.50 | $30–$60 |
| Complexity | Low | Very High |
| Gun jamming | Yes (core mechanic) | Yes (in some games) |
| Extraction via object | Microwave oven | Designated extract points |

HOLE is essentially a **solo, low-stakes, arcade-style extraction FPS** — taking the core tension of "survive and get out" without the typical punishment and complexity that defines the genre.

## Key Takeaways for PES Development

If PES is inspired by or competing with HOLE, the following design considerations are relevant:

- **The extraction mechanic is inherently tense** — use it even in low-stakes contexts.
- **Gun jams as a mechanic** create skill-based tension without requiring complex progression systems.
- **Low-resolution aesthetics** can be a feature, not a limitation.
- **Singleplayer extraction** is underexplored — HOLE proves there is an audience.
- **Infinite enemy spawns** with a "survive and extract" loop can sustain replayability.
- **Accessibility matters** — removing punishment (no loot loss) while keeping difficulty (hard gunfights) is a design worth studying.
